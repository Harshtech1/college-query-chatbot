## Design Context

### Users
Primary users are prospective and current college students who need quick, trustworthy answers without visiting the office in person. A secondary audience is the college admin or academic support staff who maintain FAQs and upload official source documents.

### Brand Personality
Confident, warm, editorial. The interface should feel dependable and institutionally credible without becoming stiff or bureaucratic.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use a confident modern, editorial campus direction. Favor light mode, warm paper tones, deep ink neutrals, and selective teal and terracotta accents. The interface should feel more like a well-designed student information desk than a generic AI app. Avoid neon gradients, dark cyber aesthetics, and generic SaaS cards.

### Design Principles
1. Lead with trust. Students should quickly understand that answers come from approved FAQs or verified documents.
2. Make status obvious. The interface should clearly distinguish between direct answers, document-backed answers, clarification prompts, and missing-information fallbacks.
3. Keep the reading experience calm. Answers, citations, and follow-up prompts should feel structured and easy to scan.
4. Give the student momentum. Suggested prompts and follow-up chips should help the user ask better questions without friction.
5. Keep admin practical. The admin experience can stay straightforward and utilitarian, but it should still feel polished and consistent with the public-facing app.
